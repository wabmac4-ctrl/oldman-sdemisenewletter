Biotechnica Denies Rumors of “Half‑Spider Person” After Witness Reports

Biotechnica issued a firm denial today after multiple residents of the Glenview Arcology reported seeing what they described as a “half‑spider person” leaping from a seventh‑story window before catching themselves mid‑fall using what appeared to be a web‑like filament. The incident, captured only in blurry, low‑light footage, has already gone viral on the DataPool. Witnesses claim the figure had elongated limbs, segmented joints, and moved with “unnatural precision.” One resident, who asked not to be named, said, “It wasn’t cyberware. I’ve seen cyberware. This was… biological.” Biotechnica responded within hours, calling the reports “baseless urban mythmaking” and insisting the corporation is not conducting any arachnid‑hybridization experiments in Night City or elsewhere. “Our research complies fully with international bioethics standards,” the statement read. Despite the denial, speculation is rampant. Some netrunners claim the creature resembles early‑stage prototypes from leaked Biotechnica black‑lab files dating back a decade. Others believe it may be a rogue experimental subject—or a heavily modified edgerunner testing new biotech. NCPD has cordoned off the area but has not confirmed whether any biological residue or cybernetic components were recovered. For now, the truth remains tangled in a web of rumor.

Cirrus Flotilla Settles In: Nomad Convoy to Remain Outside Night City

The Cirrus Flotilla, one of the largest nomad convoys operating along the Pacific corridor, has confirmed they will remain encamped outside Night City for “an extended but temporary stay.” Their sprawling caravan—hundreds of vehicles, modular rigs, and mobile workshops—has transformed the outskirts into a bustling, semi‑permanent settlement drawing curiosity, commerce, and no small amount of concern. Flotilla spokesperson Marta “Skyhook” Reyes emphasized that the group’s presence is peaceful and mutually beneficial. “We’re here to trade, repair, and rest. Night City’s economy thrives on movement, and nobody moves like nomads,” she said. Local fixers seem to agree, noting that the flotilla brings rare salvage, high‑quality mods, and skilled techs capable of repairing almost anything with wheels, wings, or wiring. Not everyone is thrilled. Several corporate‑aligned council members have raised security concerns, citing increased traffic, unauthorized drone flights, and a spike in unregistered market stalls. NCPD has issued a statement urging residents to “exercise caution” when dealing with nomad merchants, though no major incidents have been reported. Meanwhile, the Cirrus Flotilla has begun hosting open‑air concerts, food stalls, and a rotating bazaar that’s already attracting crowds from across the city. For many Night Citizens, the flotilla’s arrival feels like a welcome break from the usual chrome‑and‑concrete grind. How long they’ll stay remains unclear, but for now, the skies above Night City have a few more clouds—and a lot more color.

According to Navarro, the squad—codenamed “Ghostline”—has been quietly responsible for at least a dozen high‑precision eliminations over the past three years. Victims include rival syndicate lieutenants, corporate defectors, and at least one unnamed city official. Navarro alleges the squad uses military‑grade cyberware, clean‑room weapons, and a level of operational discipline “far beyond typical street muscle.” The Giancarlo family has issued a blanket denial, calling the claims “fabricated slander” and accusing Navarro of “seeking attention.” Sources close to the journalist say she has already received multiple threats urging her to drop the story. Navarro, however, appears undeterred. “If they wanted me scared, they should’ve tried harder,” she said in a brief holo‑statement. “Night City deserves to know who’s pulling the trigger in the dark.” Her exposé is expected to go live within the week. Security analysts predict the fallout could be explosive.
